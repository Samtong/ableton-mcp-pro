# Loop to Full Track Arrangement

## Philosophy
- Don't overthink arrangement — use your ears
- Work backwards from the drop
- Intro and outro should be DJ-friendly (don't overstuff them)

## Step 1: Place Your Main Loop
- Take your 8-bar loop and place it around bar 57 (middle of track)
- This becomes your reference point — the "drop"

## Step 2: Work Backwards — Build the Sections

### Intro (bars 1-32)
- Start with just kick
- Bass comes in halfway (bar 17), playing just ONE note (root)
- Add clap from the start
- Hi-hat enters around bar 9
- Percussion layers enter gradually
- Keep it clean for DJs — minimal effects/crashes

### Buildup (bars 33-56)
- Percussion elements
- Filter automation (e.g. vocal or synth filtering in)
- Snare build (16th notes with volume automation ramping up)
- White noise sweep with band pass filter (NOT low pass — band pass gives more control)

### Drop 1 (bars 57-72)
- Full loop with all elements
- Bass changes from one-note to full chord progression
- Add melodic screeches or additional elements

### Breakdown (bars 73-88)
- Strip back — remove kick or drums
- Keep bass or filter it out with high-pass automation
- Percussion starts to "fall apart"
- Bring back elements gradually

### Drop 2 (bars 89-104)
- Return with variations from drop 1
- Can cut kick halfway through for tension
- Bring back full bassline

### Outro (bars 105+)
- Mirror the intro in reverse — elements leave one by one
- Minimal crashes/effects for DJ mixing
- Keep it clean and simple

## Key Techniques

### Snare Build
- Take a snare sample on a new MIDI track
- 16th notes leading into drops
- Volume automation ramping up with slight curve

### Kick Impact
- Take kick sample, add heavy reverb + limiter
- One long sustained hit at transitions
- Don't use when bassline is playing (conflicts)

### Bass Filter Automation
- High-pass filter automation to make bass disappear through breakdowns
- Ramp filter up during breaks, snap back down for drops

### White Noise Sweeps
- Use any synth (Operator) with noise
- **Band pass filter** (not low pass) for controlled sweeps
- Automate filter frequency up for builds, down for drops
- Add reverb (long decay) + compressor sidechained to kick
- High-pass to keep out of mix's low end

### Crashes
- Place generously throughout — they drive arrangement more than you'd think
- Use 2+ different crash samples with different effects (delay on one, reverb on another)
- More crashes in drops/breaks, fewer in intro/outro
- Can do quick fills (short reversed crash before the main one)

## Genre-Specific Arrangement Notes

### Techno / House (DJ-focused)
- Intro and outro: 16-32 bars of just kick + percussion (essential for DJ mixing)
- Breakdowns: typically shorter (8-16 bars) — don't kill the dancefloor energy
- Drops don't need a dramatic reveal — subtle element additions work better than "everything at once"
- Typical length: 6-8 minutes

### Trance / EDM (build-and-release)
- Longer breakdowns (16-32 bars) with emotional builds
- Big dramatic drops with most elements hitting at once
- Often two distinct drops with different energy
- Typical length: 5-7 minutes

### D&B / Breakbeat
- Drops hit hard — full break + bass simultaneously
- Breakdowns often strip to just pads/atmosphere + half-time drums
- Double drops (two different sections back-to-back without breakdown) for maximum energy
- Typical length: 4-6 minutes

### Hip-Hop / Lo-Fi
- Less rigid structure — verses, hooks, bridges
- No need for DJ-friendly intros unless intended for DJ sets
- Arrangement drives the vocal/rapper — instrumental changes support lyrical sections
- Typical length: 3-4 minutes

## Build Order
1. Place main loop at drop position
2. Build backwards: intro → buildup → drop
3. Add breakdown after drop 1
4. Build drop 2 with variations
5. Create outro (reverse of intro)
6. Add effects: snare builds, white noise, crashes
7. Add filter automations on bass and other elements
