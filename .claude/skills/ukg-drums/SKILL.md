# UK Garage Drums

## Quick Reference
- **Tempo**: 130-140 BPM (classic UKG sits around 130-136; speed garage and bassline push up to 140)
- **Time Signature**: 4/4
- **Swing**: 54-60% (apply via Ableton's groove pool using an MPC 16th swing template at 60-70% depth)
- **Feel**: Crunchy, short, punchy, groovy 2-step

## Core Principle
The groove comes from **pattern placement and sound choice**, not processing. Get the kick + rimshot pattern grooving on its own before adding anything else. Once the pattern feels right, apply swing from the groove pool — MPC-style 16th swing in the 54-60% range gives the classic 2-step lilt without making it sound quantized to a rigid triplet grid.

## Sound Characteristics
- Everything is **really, really short** — short kick, short rimshot, short hats
- No big 909/808 kicks — use a short kick with little high-end
- Rimshots: use the amplitude envelope (sustain down, short decay) to tighten them
- Hi-hats: short shakers rather than open 909 hats

## Three Gain Stages (critical)
1. **Loudest**: Kick + rimshot/snare + main hi-hat
2. **Mid-level**: Background percussion (chosen so they don't fight the mains even if louder)
3. **Quietest**: Drum rack fills, constant background texture

This gain staging is what makes the main elements sound big — the quiet background elements create contrast.

## Drum Elements

### 1. Kick + Rimshot
- Short, punchy kick — pitch it up +2-3 semitones for a tighter, more energetic feel
- Rimshot on the offbeats (2-step pattern)
- Layer two rimshot samples at different velocities for a call-and-response feel; pitch the second one up +2 semitones so they sound related but distinct
- Tighten rimshot with amplitude envelope: bring sustain down, adjust decay
- Don't just crop the sample — use the envelope for a clean tail

### 2. Hi-Hats / Shakers
- Main hi-hat: a short shaker or cabasa sound, not a traditional open hat — cabasas are bright but less harsh than open hats
- Back off the attack envelope on closed hats to soften the transient — this removes the sharp "tick" and gives UKG its smoother, less house-like hat feel
- Layer a quiet 808 hi-hat underneath
- Both very short and punchy
- EQ: sweep away everything below 750 Hz on hats to keep them out of the kick's space

### 3. Background Percussion
- 2-3 percussion sounds hitting at specific syncopated points
- These create the characteristic UKG groove
- Sound choice matters — pick sounds that won't fight the mains
- Keep them softer than the main drums

### 4. Drum Rack Fills
- Constant background texture
- Quietest layer — adds movement without dominating
- Subtle fills and ghost notes

## Classic Patterns

### Pattern 1 — Classic 2-Step (2 bars)
The signature UKG feel: kicks land off the grid while the rimshot holds down beats 2 and 4. Shaker runs 8ths; ghost percussion fills the gaps.
```
Beat:   1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . . | 1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . .
Kick:   X . . . . . . . . . X . . . . . | . . . . X . . . . . . . . . X .
Rim:    . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . . | . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
Shaker: X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X . | X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
Ghost:  . . . g . . g . . g . . . g . . | . g . . . . g . . g . . . . . g
```

### Pattern 2 — Skippy 2-Step (2 bars)
Busier variant with extra displaced kicks and more ghost hits. Keep ghosts quiet — they add texture, not weight.
```
Beat:   1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . . | 1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . .
Kick:   X . . . . . X . . . X . . . . . | . . X . X . . . . . . . . . X .
Rim:    . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . . | . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
Shaker: X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X . | X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
Ghost:  . . g . . g . g . g . . . g . g | . g . g . . g . . g . g . g . .
```

### Pattern 3 — Tresillo / "Fake Triplet" (1 bar)
A dancehall-influenced rhythm common in UKG. The kick and snare hits are spaced 3-3-2 sixteenths apart (dotted 8th, dotted 8th, regular 8th), creating a rolling triplet feel without actually using triplet grid. Layer rimshots in 16th-note ghost positions between the main hits for extra groove.
```
Beat:   1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . .
Kick:   X . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .
Snare:  . . . . . . X . . . . . X . . .
Shaker: X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
Ghost:  . . . g . g . . . g . g . . . g
```
The 3-3-2 spacing (positions 1, 7, 13 in 16th notes) is the core. Variations: duplicate into 2 bars and use "triple kick / triple snare" — repeat the 3-3-2 cell with alternating kick and snare leading each group.

### Pattern 4 — Displaced Snare (2 bars)
Take the classic 2-step and shift one or both snares a 16th note early. This creates anticipation and forward momentum — a technique borrowed from drum and bass ("delayed snare"). When shifting the snare early, consider also shifting the preceding kick back slightly so the kick-to-snare spacing stays musical.
```
Beat:   1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . . | 1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . .
Kick:   X . . . . . . . . . X . . . . . | . . . . X . . . . . . . . . X .
Rim:    . . . X . . . . . . . . X . . . | . . . X . . . . . . . . X . . .
Shaker: X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X . | X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
Ghost:  . . g . . g . g . g . . . g . . | . g . . . g . g . g . . . . . g
```
The rim lands one 16th before beat 2 and one 16th before beat 4. The subtle displacement is what gives this pattern its urgency.

### Pattern 5 — Four-on-the-Floor UKG (1 bar)
Not all UKG is 2-step. The four-on-the-floor variant uses a straight kick on every quarter note (borrowed from house) but keeps the UKG character through 16th-note ghost hats, rimshot ghost notes, and swing. Use a clap on beats 2 and 4 instead of a snare — claps are more common in the 4/4 UKG variant. For bassline music, use harder kicks and claps.
```
Beat:   1 . . . 2 . . . 3 . . . 4 . . .
Kick:   X . . . X . . . X . . . X . . .
Clap:   . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
CH:     X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
OH:     . X . . . X . . . X . . . X . .
Ghost:  . . . g . . g . . . g . . . g .
```
Remember to apply 16th-note swing — without it this just sounds like house.

## Processing (via sends, not inserts)

### Send 1: Drum Bus (kick + snare)
- Cut a little low end first
- Heavy drum bus compression
- Adds body to kick and makes snare punch through
- Glues kick + snare together

### Send 2: Redux / Bit Crush (percussion + hats)
- Subtle bit crushing on all percussion via a single send
- Adds the characteristic "crunchy, fizzly" texture
- Don't put redux on individual tracks — it ruins the sound
- The send approach glues everything with the same texture

## Key Insight
The secret is:
1. **Sound choice** — short, appropriate sounds that don't fight each other
2. **Groove placement** — where the hits land matters more than processing
3. **Gain staging** — three levels of volume create depth
4. **Send processing** — glue through shared processing, not individual effects
